BlitzBox

Click here for the Repository!

BlitzBox was a team project made for University. We spent 9 weeks working together on different areas of the game. I was responsible for the networking and some gameplay areas, such as the damage, score and health systems.

I worked hard on getting the network to run smoothly and on a separate thread. I used the ENet library to make my own server and packet system. I then made a threadsafe object that was used to track the state of networked objects.

In order to make movement as smooth as possible, the states were interpolated between based on the network tick rate. I also created a buffer which would store packets and process them with a delay of around 100ms (from when they were sent). This meant that it was much less likely that packets would be dropped.

I worked closely with my team to integrate what they had been working on with the network so that everything would work smoothly. I also worked on the damage and health system, which included healing, spawn invulnerability, and different damage types.

I really enjoyed working on this project and working with my fellow teammates. I think we worked incredibly well together and we are all very happy with how the game turned out.
